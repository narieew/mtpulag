Destination Overview

The Playground
of the Gods

Mt. Pulag (Mount Pulag) is the highest peak in Luzon and the third highest mountain in the Philippines, standing at 2,928 meters above sea level. Located at the tri-boundary of Benguet, Ifugao, and Nueva Vizcaya in the Cordillera Administrative Region, it is a protected area managed by the DENR and is celebrated as a national park and ASEAN Heritage Park.

The mountain is world-renowned for its breathtaking Sea of Clouds phenomenon, where layers of clouds blanket the valleys below the summit at sunrise. Its name comes from the Ibaloi word "pulag", meaning "balding" or "grassy bald summit," referring to its iconic golden dwarf bamboo grasslands.

"I chose Mt. Pulag because it represents the pinnacle of Philippine mountaineering. Reaching the summit is not just a physical achievement but a spiritual experience — walking through mossy forests, crossing grasslands, and witnessing the sunrise above the clouds."

history_edu Brief History

Mt. Pulag has been sacred to the indigenous Ibaloi, Kalanguya, and Kankana-ey peoples for generations. Declared a national park in 1987 and an ASEAN Heritage Park in 2014, it protects diverse ecosystems from pine forests to mossy forests and grasslands, home to the endangered Philippine eagle and cloud rat.

Hiking Schedule

The Ambangeg
Expedition Timeline

Mt. Pulag requires careful timing to catch the sunrise above the Sea of Clouds. This schedule follows the standard 2-day, 1-night Ambangeg Trail itinerary — the most beginner-friendly route to the summit.

schedule 2 Days / 1 Night • Ambangeg Trail
Day 1 • 06:00 AM

Depart Manila / Baguio

Travel to Baguio City, then proceed to Kabayan, Benguet via van or bus.

Day 1 • 10:00 AM

DENR Registration

Register at the Mt. Pulag Protected Area Office. Secure guide and briefing.

Day 1 • 01:00 PM

Start Trek — Ranger Station

Begin ascent from Ambangeg Ranger Station through pine and mossy forests.

Day 1 • 04:00 PM

Arrive at Camp Site

Set up camp at designated area. Early dinner and rest before summit push.

Day 2 • 01:00 AM

Wake Up & Prepare

Early breakfast and layering. Check headlamp and warm gear.

Day 2 • 02:30 AM

Summit Push Begins

Ascent in darkness through dwarf bamboo slopes. Headlamps mandatory.

Day 2 • 05:30 AM

Summit — 2,928m

Arrive at Peak 3. Witness the legendary Sea of Clouds at sunrise.

Day 2 • 08:00 AM

Descent & Return

Descend to camp, break down, and trek back to Ranger Station. Depart for home.

Climate Conditions

Weather at Mt. Pulag

High-altitude conditions demand proper preparation. Temperatures can drop dramatically, especially before sunrise.

ac_unit

Cold Climate

0–10°C

Summit temperatures regularly drop near freezing. Wind chill makes it feel colder.

cloud

Sea of Clouds

Best viewed at sunrise (5:00–6:30 AM) when temperature inversions trap clouds in valleys below.

calendar_month

Best Hiking Months

November to February (dry season) offer clearest skies. Avoid typhoon season (July–October).

air

Wind Conditions

Strong gusts at the summit and grasslands. Bring a windproof shell jacket and secure loose items.

rainy

Rain Preparation

Sudden rain is common. Pack waterproof jacket, rain cover for backpack, and dry bags for electronics and clothes.

wb_twilight

Morning Conditions

Pre-dawn treks are coldest and darkest. Layer up, use headlamp, and trek with your guide group.

Ambangeg Trail

Through Three Worlds

The changing ecosystems of Mt. Pulag

route Trail Overview

  • Starting Point: Ambangeg Ranger Station, Bokod-Kabayan Road
  • Registration: DENR Protected Area Office (required before trek)
  • Checkpoint: Camp 1 & Camp 2 rest areas along trail
  • Camp Site: Designated camping area before summit push
  • Summit: Peak 3 (2,928m) — main viewpoint for Sea of Clouds
  • Difficulty: 3/9 (Beginner-friendly)
  • Duration: 4–5 hours ascent, 2–3 hours descent
  • Terrain: Well-established dirt trails, gradual inclines, some muddy sections

Safety Protocols

emergency_home Safety & Survival

Hypothermia & Cold Exposure

Due to high altitude and wind chill, hypothermia is a serious risk at Mt. Pulag. Watch for shivering, confusion, slurred speech, and loss of coordination. Avoid cotton clothing — it traps sweat and freezes.

Weather Reminders

Personal Journal

Traveler's Reflection

"Reaching Mt. Pulag reminded me that the greatest adventures are not always about conquering difficult terrain — they are about patience, discipline, and deep appreciation for nature. Standing above the Sea of Clouds at 2,928 meters, I felt humbled by the beauty of the Cordilleras and grateful for every step that brought me there."

Physical Challenges

Managing thin air, steep inclines, and freezing temperatures at the summit required mental toughness, steady pacing, and proper layering to avoid exhaustion and hypothermia.

Mental Challenges

Overcoming sleep deprivation during the 2:30 AM summit push was the hardest part. Pushing through darkness and cold demanded focus, teamwork, and trust in our guide.

Lessons Learned

I learned that protecting mountains is everyone's responsibility. Leave No Trace is not optional — it ensures future generations can experience the same breathtaking beauty we did.

Favorite Moment

Watching the first golden rays of sunlight illuminate the endless Sea of Clouds from Luzon's highest peak. In that moment, every early wake-up and every cold step felt completely worth it.
